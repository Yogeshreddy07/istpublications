Publication Guidelines & Ethics

Ensuring the highest standards of integrity, quality, and transparency in scientific publishing.

Publication Ethics

Innovative Science and Technology Publications Group (ISTPublications) is committed to peer review integrity and practices the highest standards of ethical publishing across all ISTP journals. We publish original research papers that have not been published nor are under review elsewhere.

IST publication ethics encourages integrity in the research and peer review process as outlined below, and we prohibit any malpractices regarding publication. ISTPublications uses a hybrid tool to verify the originality of every submitted manuscript. We promote new research and scientific applications in the field of Computer Science & Engineering and Technology with Allied fields, providing high-quality and flexible information solutions to researchers.

Principal Advice to Authors

Fabrication/Falsification of Data

Making up data or results and recording or reporting them is fabrication of data. Falsification of data is manipulating research materials, omitting, deletion, or suppression of conflicting data without justification. This causes mistrust and disgrace to the scientific community.

Plagiarism

Using another person’s ideas, language, graphs, pictures, results, and experiments without giving credit to them. Without citing the source, copying even a single sentence from your own or another person’s research paper leads to plagiarism.

Unacceptable Author Contribution

Including authors who have not contributed to the research in the manuscript and excluding authors who have contributed to the research is unacceptable author contribution.

Citation Manipulation

Citing irrelevant articles that don’t contribute to the content of the manuscript is a kind of scientific misconduct.

Concurrent Submissions

Submitting the same manuscript to more than one journal at the same time. It is a waste of time for editors and peer reviewers and can also damage the reputation of journals if published in more than one place.

Redundant Publications

This primarily refers to publishing the same material/experiments more than once. Accountability: All authors whose names appear on the manuscript are equally held accountable for the content of a submitted manuscript or published paper.

Ethical Guidelines to Reviewers

IST Publications journals are peer-reviewed by two or three reviewers, and the Editor-in-Chief makes the final decision. It is expected that reviewers during the process should be principled, prompt, act with confidence, contribute to the final decision, acknowledge sources, and stick to the objectives below:

  • Reviewers should understand that the peer review process is confidential. The review process should not be shared with anyone outside the peer review process.
  • Reviewers clearly understand that the review process should be unbiased and the author deserves full credit for their work.
  • Reviewers should submit a comprehensive and substantial peer review report in a timely manner. If there is a delay, it should be communicated to the editor.
  • Reviewers must communicate to the Editor-in-Chief if the papers are plagiarized or published elsewhere.

Rights of Chief Editorial Board

The Editor-in-Chief makes the final decision to publish the manuscript in the journal based on peer-reviewer comments. Editors should be principled, prompt, and stick to the following guidelines:

  • Editors should understand that the peer review process is confidential and should not be shared.
  • Editors clearly understand that the review process should be unbiased and the author deserves full credit for their work.
  • Editor-in-Chief should discuss all matters regarding publication with the Editorial Board members before making a final decision.
  • Editors should automatically reject manuscripts that are inappropriate or out of the scope of the journal.
  • Editors must investigate if they receive information that a manuscript is plagiarized, under consideration elsewhere, or has already been published. If confirmed, the Editor-in-Chief should issue an erratum.
